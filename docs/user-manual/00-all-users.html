1. What This System Does

The KSB Purchase Requisition System replaces paper-based procurement and stores paperwork with one web application used by every department of KSB Zambia.

1.1 What you can do in the system

  • Purchase Requisitions — ask the company to buy something on your behalf.
  • EFT Requisitions — request an electronic transfer to a supplier or person.
  • Petty Cash Requisitions — draw a small amount of cash for immediate purchases.
  • Expense Claims — get reimbursed for money you spent on company business.
  • Goods Receipt Notes (GRNs) — record stock arriving at the warehouse.
  • Issue Slips — record stock leaving stores to departments or sites.
  • Picking Slips — record stock being moved between storage locations.
  • Stock Register — see live available stock at any time.

1.2 Approval flow at a glance

Purchase Requisition (typical flow) [Initiator] ----submit----> [Pending HOD] | approve ---+--- reject -----> [Rejected] v [Pending Finance] | approve ---+--- reject -----> [Rejected] v [Pending MD] | approve ---+--- reject -----> [Rejected] v [Approved] EFT / Petty Cash / Expense Claim / Issue Slip skip the MD step. GRN has a single Finance-approval step. Picking Slip has no approval — it is created as Completed.
Your role decides which forms appear on your screen and which approval buttons you can click. The sections below are the same for everyone; role-specific detail lives in each role's manual.

2. Logging In for the First Time

2.1 Before You Log In

  1. Ask your Administrator for the system URL (usually something like https://pras-frontend.onrender.com).
  2. Ask for your username and a temporary password.
  3. Confirm with them that your role (initiator, HOD, Finance, etc.) and department are set correctly before you try to log in.

2.2 Log In

  1. Open a modern web browser — Chrome, Edge, or Firefox is recommended.
  2. Go to the system URL given by your Administrator.
  3. Type your Username.
  4. Type your Password.
  5. Click Login.
  6. If the credentials are correct, you will land on the Dashboard.
Login screen showing username field, password field, and Login button.

2.3 Change Your Temporary Password

  1. After first login, click your name in the top-right corner.
  2. Select Profile (or Change Password if available).
  3. Enter your current temporary password, then your new password twice.
  4. The new password must be at least 8 characters and contain an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, and a digit.
  5. Click Save.
Never share your password, even with IT. A real Administrator will never ask you for your password — they reset it instead.

3. The Screen Layout

Every page of the system uses the same three-part layout.

+-----------------------------------------------------------+ | TOP BAR: KSB logo Dashboard > My Requisitions [You] | +-----------+-----------------------------------------------+ | | | | SIDEBAR | MAIN AREA | | | (forms, lists, approvals, dashboards) | | Menu | | | items | | | grouped | | | by | | | section | | | | | +-----------+-----------------------------------------------+

3.1 Top Bar

  • Shows your full name and role on the right.
  • Click your name to open the Profile / Log Out menu.
  • Shows a breadcrumb of where you currently are (e.g. "Dashboard > My Requisitions").

3.2 Sidebar

  • The main menu. Grouped by category (Procurement, Financial Forms, Stores, etc.).
  • Items are hidden if your role does not have access. If you can't see something, your role probably doesn't allow it.
  • Click a grouping header to collapse or expand it.

3.3 Main Area

  • Shows the page you clicked — a list, a form, or a detail view.
  • At the top of a detail page, you'll usually find Download PDF, Print, or Edit buttons.
  • At the bottom of an approval page, you'll find Approve / Reject with a comments box.

5. Logging Out and Session Timeouts

5.1 Log Out Manually

  1. Click your name in the top-right corner.
  2. Select Logout.
  3. You will be returned to the login screen.

5.2 Automatic Logout

  1. If you leave the system idle for about 15 minutes, your session will expire.
  2. On your next action you will be asked to log in again.
  3. Work in any form that you haven't submitted will be lost — submit regularly rather than keeping drafts open for hours.
Always log out before leaving a shared computer. If you stay logged in, anyone walking past can approve or create forms as you.

6. Security Rules

  • Password length: minimum 8 characters with uppercase, lowercase, and at least one digit.
  • Brute-force protection: after 20 failed login attempts from the same computer in 15 minutes, further attempts are blocked for 15 minutes.
  • Audit logging: every approval, rejection, and critical status change is recorded with your name, IP, browser, and timestamp. You can be held accountable for actions taken from your account.
  • Secure transport: all data is sent over HTTPS.

7. Troubleshooting — Login Problems

7.1 "Invalid username or password. Make sure backend server is running."

  1. Double-check caps lock and your typing.
  2. Ask your Administrator to reset your password.
  3. If a colleague reports the same error at the same time, the backend may be down. Contact IT.

7.2 "Too many login attempts from this IP"

  1. Wait 15 minutes.
  2. Try once only — more failures restart the timer.
  3. If this keeps happening, tell IT. Someone on your network may be trying to guess a password.

7.3 Login succeeds but I see a mostly empty page

  1. Hard-refresh the page: Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac).
  2. Log out and log back in.
  3. If still empty, your role may not have access to anything. Ask your Administrator.

7.4 "Session expired, please login again"

Normal after 15 minutes of inactivity. Log in again.
