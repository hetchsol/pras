1. Who You Are

You are a Finance user (officer or manager). You act as the second-stage gatekeeper after HODs and you control budgets and foreign-exchange rates used across the whole system.

1.1 What you can do

  • Approve / reject any request at pending_finance.
  • Approve GRNs (goods receipts) so stock is officially in the system.
  • See and edit department budgets.
  • See and edit currency FX rates.
  • See every request in the system for reporting.
  • Run financial analytics reports.

1.2 What you cannot do

  • You cannot skip the HOD stage. Requests must reach you via HOD approval.
  • You cannot approve your own initiated requests.
  • You cannot delete users or change system roles (that is the Administrator's job).
  • You cannot modify an approved request. If something is wrong after approval, create a new one with a correction.

1.3 Your responsibilities

  1. Check the request sits within the department budget.
  2. Check the request has the required supporting documents (quote, invoice, receipt).
  3. For foreign-currency items, verify the FX conversion is reasonable.
  4. Keep FX rates up to date (weekly at minimum).
  5. Approve GRNs promptly so stock becomes usable for the operations team.

2. Quick Reference Card

One-page cheat sheet

To approve PRs: Sidebar → Procurement → Pending Approvals.

To approve EFT / PC / EC: Sidebar → Financial Forms → Pending Approvals.

To approve GRNs: Sidebar → Stores → Goods Receipt Notes, filter pending_approval.

To approve Issue Slips: Sidebar → Stores → Issue Slips, filter pending_finance.

Budgets: Sidebar → Financial Planning → Budgets.

FX Rates: Sidebar → Financial Planning → FX Rates.

What to check before approving

  1. Is the department under its budget?
  2. Is the supplier invoice or quote attached?
  3. Does the amount match the supporting document?
  4. Is the FX conversion (if applicable) using the current rate?
  5. Did the HOD leave a meaningful comment? If the HOD approved in silence, ask for context before acting.

Emergency escalation

  • Time-critical payments can be marked urgent in the comment; MD will prioritise.

3. Approval Workflow from Your Point of View

[HOD approved] | v +--------------------------+ | STATUS = pending_finance | <-- your queue +--------------------------+ | approve / reject | +-------------+-------------+ | | v v pending_md approved (only if PR value (EFT, PC, EC, > MD threshold) Issue Slip, or low-value PR) GRN workflow (separate): [Stores creates GRN] --> pending_approval --> approved ^ | YOU approve / reject

3.1 When you approve a Purchase Requisition

  • If the total exceeds the MD threshold (check your local configuration), status becomes pending_md.
  • Otherwise, status becomes approved directly and Procurement takes over.

3.2 When you approve an EFT / Petty Cash / Expense Claim

  • Status becomes approved. No MD stage.
  • For EFT, Finance then processes the actual bank payment outside the system.

3.3 When you approve a GRN

  • Stock becomes officially available in the Stock Register.
  • Related Issue Slips can now draw from this GRN.

5. Task 1 — Review and Approve Financial Requests

5.1 Open the Pending Approvals list

  1. For Purchase Requisitions: Sidebar → Procurement → Pending Approvals.
  2. For EFT / Petty Cash / Expense Claims: Sidebar → Financial Forms → Pending Approvals.

5.2 Click a request to open the detail view

  1. Check the HOD has approved — the Approval History section should show an HOD entry.
  2. Read the HOD's comment for context.
Approval history section showing HOD entry approved, Finance entry pending.

5.3 Check budget impact

  1. Sidebar → Financial Planning → Budgets (open in a new tab so you keep the request open).
  2. Find the department of the request.
  3. Check Budget and Spent columns.
  4. Will this request push the department over budget? If yes, you may need to escalate or reject.

5.4 Check supporting documents

  1. For a PR, the attached supplier quote should match the unit prices.
  2. For an EFT, the invoice should match the amount and the payee bank details.
  3. For Petty Cash, the itemised breakdown should make sense.
  4. For an Expense Claim, every line should have a receipt attached.

5.5 Check FX (if applicable)

  1. If the request is in USD, EUR, GBP, or ZAR, confirm the ZMW-equivalent shown on the detail page uses a recent FX rate.
  2. If the rate looks stale, update it (see Task 5) before approving. The system re-converts automatically.

5. Task 1 — continued

5.6 Approve or reject

  1. Write a comment. Examples:
    • Approve: "Within Q2 budget. Quote from Tech Solutions verified."
    • Approve with note: "Approved. Flag to MD — this is the last PR before hitting IT budget cap."
    • Reject: "Invoice amount does not match the form (K5200 vs K5020). Please correct and resubmit."
  2. Click Approve or Reject.
  3. On approval: status moves to pending_md (for high-value PR) or approved.
  4. On rejection: status becomes rejected, initiator is notified.
Never approve a request where the supporting document does not match the form. Discrepancies are your responsibility to catch — the audit log will show you approved despite the mismatch.

6. Task 2 — Approve a GRN

When Stores receive goods, they create a Goods Receipt Note (GRN) at status pending_approval. Your role is to verify and approve.

6.1 Open pending GRNs

  1. Sidebar → Stores → Goods Receipt Notes.
  2. Filter or sort by Status → pending_approval.
  3. Click a GRN to open its detail.

6.2 Verify quantities

  1. Note the Reference PR — the purchase requisition this GRN relates to.
  2. Compare the Quantity Received on the GRN against the PR's ordered quantities.
  3. If the GRN is a partial delivery, that's fine — another GRN can follow when the balance arrives.

6.3 Verify supplier match

  1. Does the Supplier / Vendor match the one selected on the PR?
  2. If different, check whether a vendor substitution was approved (via Procurement).

6.4 Approve or reject

  1. Write a comment (e.g. "Quantities verified against delivery note DN-2026-0154").
  2. Click Approve.
  3. Status becomes approved. Goods are now officially in stock and available for issue slips.
If another approver acts on the same GRN at the same moment, only one approval succeeds. The later one will see a 409 Conflict message. Simply refresh and the status will already show approved.
GRN detail page with Approve / Reject buttons at the bottom.

7. Task 3 — Approve an Issue Slip (Finance stage)

7.1 Find pending Issue Slips

  1. Sidebar → Stores → Issue Slips.
  2. Filter by status pending_finance.

7.2 Verify stock availability

  1. Open the Issue Slip.
  2. Note the Reference Number (GRN this stock came from).
  3. The system automatically checks remaining stock — you do not need to manually calculate.
  4. If the Issue Slip reached this stage without errors, availability has already been verified.

7.3 Verify customer reservation (if applicable)

  1. If the GRN was reserved for a specific customer, the Issue Slip's Customer field must match.
  2. The system enforces this at creation, but double-check on high-value issues.

7.4 Approve

  1. Write a comment.
  2. Click Approve.
  3. Status becomes approved. Stock is officially deducted from the register.

8. Task 4 — Maintain Budgets

8.1 Open the Budgets page

  1. Sidebar → Financial Planning → Budgets.
  2. The page lists every department with its annual budget and spend-to-date.

8.2 Edit a department's budget

  1. Click the department row.
  2. Enter the new budget amount in ZMW.
  3. Click Save.
  4. The change takes effect immediately. Future requests are compared to the new number.

8.3 Year-end rollover

  1. At the end of the financial year, reset each department's Spent to zero using the rollover tool (if available) or by coordinating with Administration.
  2. Set the new year's budget values at the same time.
  3. Document the rollover in a comment visible to MD and Admin.
Budgets are for your visibility. The system does not currently block requests that exceed budget — it's a soft control. You must enforce it manually during approval review.

9. Task 5 — Maintain FX Rates

9.1 Open FX Rates

  1. Sidebar → Financial Planning → FX Rates.
  2. See the current rate for each supported currency: USD, EUR, GBP, ZAR.

9.2 Update a rate

  1. Click the row of the currency.
  2. Enter the new Rate to ZMW — how many ZMW equal one unit of that currency.
  3. Click Save.
  4. The change is effective immediately; any pending request in that currency will show the new ZMW value on refresh.

9.3 Suggested cadence

  1. Monday: update all four currencies using the Bank of Zambia midweek rate.
  2. Update ad-hoc if a rate moves by more than 2% during the week.
  3. Document big movements in a comment so MD can see why expensive requests changed value.
FX Rates page with currency rows: ZMW (1.00), USD (27.5), EUR (29.5), GBP (34.5), ZAR (1.5).

10. Task 6 — Run Financial Reports

10.1 Summary Reports

  1. Sidebar → Reports & Analytics → Reports.
  2. Pick a date range (Last 30 Days, This Month, This Year, etc.).
  3. See total requests, approvals, rejections, and average turnaround.
  4. Export to Excel or PDF.

10.2 Analytics

  1. Sidebar → Reports & Analytics → Analytics.
  2. See deeper charts: spend by department, vendor concentration, approval bottlenecks.
  3. Use the filters at the top to narrow by date, department, or status.
  4. This page is read-only — use it in your monthly finance review.

10.3 Individual form PDFs

  1. Open any approved document's detail page.
  2. Click Download PDF.
  3. The PDF includes header, items, approval signatures, and the KSB letterhead.

11. Troubleshooting

11.1 The ZMW amount on a PR looks wrong

  1. Check the FX Rates page — is the currency rate up to date?
  2. Update the rate, then refresh the PR detail page.
  3. If still wrong, check the item has the correct currency selected.

11.2 I can't approve a GRN — the button is greyed out

  1. Check your role includes Finance, or that you are the explicitly assigned approver on the GRN.
  2. Check the GRN isn't already approved — refresh.
  3. If you see a 409 Conflict error, another approver acted first — refresh to see the updated status.

11.3 Department "Spent" looks wrong

  1. The Spent figure is computed from all approved requisitions against that department.
  2. Rejected requests do not count.
  3. If a recent large approval isn't reflected, wait a few seconds and refresh — the dashboard may be cached.

11.4 A request is at pending_finance but I don't see it

  1. Check the combined Pending Approvals list.
  2. Filter out by form type to narrow down.
  3. If still missing, Admin may need to re-run index sync.

  • All FX rates reviewed and updated weekly.
