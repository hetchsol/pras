1. Who You Are

You are an Initiator — any staff member who needs the company to spend money on their behalf. You don't need any special role flag; every employee can initiate.

1.1 What you can do

  • Create a Purchase Requisition when you need goods or services.
  • Create an EFT Requisition when the company needs to pay someone by bank transfer.
  • Create a Petty Cash Requisition when you need a small amount of cash.
  • Create an Expense Claim to be reimbursed for money you already spent.
  • View the status of all your own requests.
  • See your own history — what you have initiated, approved amounts, dates.

1.2 What you cannot do

  • You cannot approve or reject any request, even your own.
  • You cannot see other people's requests unless the HOD or Admin gives you a wider role.
  • You cannot edit a request after it is submitted. If it is wrong, cancel by asking an approver to reject it, or submit a new one.

1.3 Who approves your requests

  1. Your Head of Department (HOD) is the first approver. Your HOD is set on your user profile — ask your Administrator if you are not sure who it is.
  2. After HOD approval, the request moves to Finance.
  3. For high-value Purchase Requisitions, the final step is MD approval. Expense claims, petty cash, and EFT usually stop at Finance.

2. Quick Reference Card

One-page cheat sheet

To buy something: Sidebar → Procurement → Create Requisition.

To pay a supplier: Sidebar → Financial Forms → EFT Requisitions → Create New.

To draw cash: Sidebar → Financial Forms → Petty Cash → Create New.

To claim reimbursement: Sidebar → Financial Forms → Expense Claims → Create New.

To track a request: Sidebar → the relevant list. Look at the Status column.

Meaning of each status

  • pending_hod At your HOD, not yet approved.
  • pending_finance HOD approved, Finance is next.
  • pending_md Finance approved, MD is next (high-value PRs).
  • approved Fully approved — procurement will action it.
  • rejected Rejected — read the comments.

When in doubt

  • Type a clear title and justification. Vague requests get rejected.
  • Add a supplier quote if you have one. Faster approval.
  • Use the Required Date to signal urgency — but be realistic.

3. Approval Workflow Diagrams

3.1 Purchase Requisition

[You] submit --> pending_hod | approve / reject v pending_finance | approve / reject v pending_md (only if value > MD threshold) | approve / reject v approved

3.2 EFT / Petty Cash / Expense Claim

[You] submit --> pending_hod --> pending_finance --> approved MD is NOT in this chain.

3.3 What "rejected" means

At any step, the approver may reject the request. If that happens:

  • Status becomes rejected.
  • The rejection comment appears on the detail page.
  • The request cannot be resubmitted. You must create a new one with the correction.
  • You do not get a new request-ID from the rejected one. Each submission is its own record.
The audit log keeps a permanent record of every submission and every rejection, with the reason. A rejected request is not "gone" — Finance and Admin can still see it under "Rejected Requisitions".

5. Task 1 — Create a Purchase Requisition

Use a Purchase Requisition (PR) when you want the company to buy goods or services for you or for your department.

5.1 Open the PR form

  1. In the sidebar, click Procurement to expand that section.
  2. Click Create Requisition.
  3. The form opens — it may open in a new tab.
Purchase Requisition form (empty) showing Title, Description, Delivery Location, Required Date, and Items table.

5.2 Fill in the header

  1. Title — a short name (e.g. "Office printer toner x 3").
  2. Description — a sentence or two explaining what you need and why.
  3. Department — usually filled from your profile. Change only if requesting for another department with their agreement.
  4. Delivery Location — where the goods should go: Office, Site, Workshop, etc.
  5. Required Date — the date by which you need the items.
  6. Currency — default is ZMW. Change if the supplier quotes in USD, EUR, GBP, or ZAR.

5.3 Add each item

  1. Click Add Item.
  2. Fill in for each line:
    • Item Name (required)
    • Item Code (if you know it — optional)
    • Description (optional but helpful for clarity)
    • Quantity (must be 1 or more)
    • Unit (pcs, kg, box, litre, hour, etc.)
    • Estimated Unit Price (if you have a quote; otherwise leave blank)
  3. Click Add Item again for each additional line.
  4. To remove a line, click the delete icon on its row.

5.4 Add justification and attachments

  1. Scroll to the Justification text box.
  2. Explain why this purchase is needed. Be specific — approvers read this first.
  3. If you have a supplier quote, click Attach File and upload the PDF or image. Maximum 10 MB.
Supported formats are PDF, JPG, PNG, Excel, and Word.

5.5 Review and submit

  1. Scroll up and check the title, items, prices, and justification one more time.
  2. Click Submit at the bottom.
  3. The system creates a requisition with an ID in the format KSB-PR-<timestamp>.
  4. Status becomes pending_hod.
  5. Your HOD receives an email notification.
  6. You are redirected back to the "My Requisitions" list where you can see the new entry.
"My Requisitions" list showing the newly-created requisition row with status "pending_hod".

5.6 Common mistakes that cause rejection

  1. No justification — approvers reject these almost always. Fill the box.
  2. Wrong department — if you accidentally picked another department, Finance cannot charge the budget properly.
  3. Missing quote for high-value items — anything > the unquoted limit (check with your HOD or Finance) usually needs a quote.
  4. Vague item names — "miscellaneous parts" is too vague. Name each specific part.
  5. Duplicate line items — check you haven't added the same thing twice.
Once submitted you cannot edit the PR. If you notice a mistake, ask your HOD to reject it with a comment. Then create a new PR.

6. Task 2 — Create an EFT Requisition

Use an EFT Requisition when the company needs to pay a supplier or an individual by electronic bank transfer.

6.1 Open the EFT form

  1. Sidebar → Financial Forms → EFT Requisitions.
  2. Click Create New EFT (or similar).

6.2 Fill in the form

  1. Payee Name — who is being paid.
  2. Bank Name — payee's bank.
  3. Account Number / Branch — as on the payee's invoice.
  4. Amount and Currency.
  5. Purpose — short description of why you're paying (e.g. "Settlement of invoice INV-2026-0042 for IT supplies").
  6. Reference — invoice or PO number.
  7. Attachment — upload the supplier invoice or supporting document.
EFT Requisition form with sections for Payee details, Amount, and Attachment.

6.3 Submit

  1. Click Submit.
  2. ID format: KSB-EFT-<timestamp>.
  3. Status becomes pending_hod.
  4. Finance will receive it after your HOD approves.
Double-check bank details before submitting. A wrong account number leads to delayed or misdirected payments.

7. Task 3 — Create a Petty Cash Requisition

Use this to draw a small amount of cash from the petty-cash float for immediate purchases (tea, office stationery, minor repairs).

7.1 Open the Petty Cash form

  1. Sidebar → Financial Forms → Petty Cash.
  2. Click Create New Petty Cash Request.

7.2 Fill in the form

  1. Amount and Currency (usually ZMW).
  2. Purpose — one-line reason for the cash.
  3. Itemised breakdown — list what you will buy with the cash. Add a line for each item.
  4. Required By — the date by which you need the cash.

7.3 Submit

  1. Click Submit.
  2. ID format: KSB-PC-<timestamp>.
  3. Status: pending_hod.
Keep your receipts after spending the petty cash. Finance may ask you to submit them as a reconciliation. Missing receipts can result in the amount being deducted from your next pay.

8. Task 4 — Create an Expense Claim

Use this when you have already spent your own money on company business and want to be reimbursed.

8.1 Open the Expense Claim form

  1. Sidebar → Financial Forms → Expense Claims.
  2. Click Create New Claim.

8.2 Fill in the header

  1. Claim Title — e.g. "Travel to Ndola 15 April 2026".
  2. Claim Date — today's date.
  3. Currency — usually ZMW.

8.3 Add each expense line

  1. Click Add Expense Line.
  2. For each line, enter:
    • Date the expense was incurred.
    • Category — Travel, Meals, Accommodation, Fuel, Other.
    • Description — brief detail (e.g. "Fuel Lusaka to Ndola, 420 km").
    • Amount — the amount you paid.
    • Receipt — attach a photo or scan.
  3. Add as many lines as you need. The total is calculated at the bottom.
Expense Claim form showing three expense lines — Fuel, Accommodation, Meals — with attached receipts.

8.4 Submit

  1. Click Submit.
  2. ID format: KSB-EXP-<timestamp>.
  3. Status: pending_hod.
Keep your original paper receipts for at least six months. Tax or audit staff may ask for them.

9. Task 5 — Track Your Requests

At any time you can check where your submissions are in the approval chain.

9.1 Open the relevant list

  1. For Purchase Requisitions: Sidebar → Procurement → My Requisitions.
  2. For EFT: Sidebar → Financial Forms → EFT Requisitions.
  3. For Petty Cash: Sidebar → Financial Forms → Petty Cash.
  4. For Expense Claims: Sidebar → Financial Forms → Expense Claims.

9.2 Read the status column

  1. Each row shows the request ID, date, a short title, and the Status column.
  2. The status matches one of the values in the Quick Reference card.
  3. Click any row to open the full detail page.

9.3 Read the full history

  1. On the detail page, scroll to Approval History (sometimes called Approvals or Audit).
  2. Each entry shows: who acted, their role, what they did (approved / rejected / pending), the comment they left, and the date/time.
  3. This gives you a clear trail of where it's been and where it's going.
Requisition detail page showing Approval History: HOD approved, Finance pending.

9.4 Download the PDF copy

  1. On the detail page, click Download PDF (top-right).
  2. The PDF contains the header, items, and approval signatures. Print or email as needed.
  3. PDF is available even while the request is still pending.

10. Task 6 — Respond to a Rejection

If an approver rejects your request, follow these steps.

10.1 Read why it was rejected

  1. Open the rejected request from the list (status rejected).
  2. Read the rejection comment in the Approval History. It should tell you what is wrong.
  3. If the comment is unclear, contact the person who rejected it directly.

10.2 Create a corrected version

  1. You cannot edit a rejected request. You must create a new one.
  2. Start a new form of the same type (e.g. new PR).
  3. Fix the issue: rephrase the justification, add a quote, correct a quantity, change the item name, etc.
  4. In the description, reference the rejected request's ID for context (e.g. "Resubmitted from KSB-PR-20260421... with added quote").
  5. Submit.

10.3 If you think the rejection is wrong

  1. Talk to the rejector face-to-face or by phone.
  2. If you cannot agree, escalate to your HOD (if rejected by HOD, escalate to Finance).
  3. Only resubmit after the disagreement is resolved.
Repeated resubmissions without addressing the feedback are visible to all approvers and damage trust. Fix the issue first.

11. Troubleshooting

11.1 I submitted the form but nothing appears in "My Requisitions"

  1. Refresh the page.
  2. Check that you are looking at the right list. Purchase Requisitions appear under Procurement, not Financial Forms.
  3. Check that you didn't switch departments after creating — the list may be filtered.

11.2 File upload failed

  1. Make sure the file is under 10 MB.
  2. Use a supported format: PDF, JPG, PNG, Excel, or Word.
  3. Try renaming the file to remove special characters (&, #, %).

11.3 The "Required Date" won't accept my input

  1. Use the date picker (calendar icon) rather than typing.
  2. Cannot pick a date in the past.

11.4 I see an error "Insufficient permissions"

  1. You are trying to do something your role doesn't allow.
  2. If you believe the role is wrong, ask your Administrator to check.

11.5 I can't find my HOD's name in any dropdown

  1. HOD is set on your profile, not selected by you. Ask your Administrator to assign your HOD correctly.

11.6 I don't receive email confirmations

  1. Check your spam/junk folder.
  2. Confirm your email address is correct on your user profile.
  3. If still nothing, tell IT — the mail service may be down.
