1. Who You Are

You are the Managing Director. In this system, your role is to give final approval on Purchase Requisitions whose value exceeds the delegated threshold set by Finance.

1.1 What you can do

  • Approve / reject Purchase Requisitions at pending_md.
  • See every request in the system across all departments.
  • Access all reports and analytics.
  • Initiate your own requests if you need to.

1.2 What you will not typically do

  • EFT, Petty Cash, and Expense Claim forms stop at Finance and do not reach you.
  • GRNs are handled by Finance, not you.
  • User management is the Administrator's responsibility.

1.3 What you should focus on

  1. Strategic fit — does this purchase align with company direction?
  2. Value for money — is the winning quote demonstrably the best option?
  3. Budget impact — does it leave room for other known upcoming spend?
  4. Risk — any supplier or compliance concerns?
  5. Urgency — is there a good reason this must be approved now vs next month?
Because most operational detail was already checked by HOD and Finance, your review is about oversight, not transaction detail. If the numbers already add up, you are being asked: "Is this the right thing to spend company money on?"

2. Quick Reference Card

One-page cheat sheet

To approve: Sidebar → Procurement → Pending Approvals.

Only PRs reach you — no EFTs, petty cash, expense claims, or stores forms.

The list shows only PRs at status pending_md.

Read the prior history: HOD and Finance have already commented. Their views matter.

Questions to ask before approving

  1. Is this strategically aligned with our goals?
  2. Has Finance confirmed the department has budget headroom?
  3. Are there competing requests this month that I should prioritise against?
  4. Did the HOD's comment reveal concerns Finance glossed over?
  5. Is the supplier a known good one, or a new one I want to question?

If you need more info

  • Don't approve blind. Call the HOD or Finance directly.
  • Leave the request pending while you get the answer.

3. Approval Workflow from Your Point of View

[HOD approved] [Finance approved] | | v v pending_finance pending_md <-- your queue | approve / reject | +----------------+----------------+ | | v v approved rejected (goes to Procurement (returns to initiator for vendor order) with your comment)

3.1 When does a PR reach you?

  • Only when total value exceeds the MD threshold configured by Finance.
  • Below the threshold, Finance approves and the PR is done.
  • You can view all PRs, but only act on the ones at pending_md.

3.2 What happens after your approval

  • Status becomes approved.
  • Procurement receives the signal to raise the purchase order with the selected vendor.
  • Initiator and HOD receive an email.

3.3 What happens after your rejection

  • Status becomes rejected.
  • Initiator and earlier approvers see your comment.
  • No further action is automatic — the initiator decides whether to rework and resubmit.

5. Task 1 — Find Requests Waiting for You

5.1 Dashboard card

  1. On the Dashboard, the Pending Approvals card shows the count of PRs waiting for you.
  2. Click the card to open the list directly.

5.2 Procurement Pending Approvals

  1. Sidebar → Procurement → Pending Approvals.
  2. The list is already filtered to pending_md.
  3. Columns shown: ID, initiator, department, total amount, date of Finance approval.
  4. Sort by amount (descending) if you want to action the largest first.
MD pending approvals list sorted by amount, largest first.

5.3 Filter by urgency

  1. Use the Required Date column to spot time-critical items.
  2. Requests with a Required Date within the next 7 days should be reviewed first.

6. Task 2 — Review a High-Value Request

6.1 Open the detail view

  1. Click the request row.
  2. Scan the header: title, initiator, department, total, required date.

6.2 Read the Approval History carefully

  1. Scroll to Approval History (also called Audit or Approvals).
  2. Read the HOD entry — what did they say?
  3. Read the Finance entry — any flags, comments, or conditions?
  4. If any prior comment says "approved but…", treat the "but" as a concern for you to resolve.

6.3 Check supplier / adjudication

  1. Does the request name a single supplier, or was an adjudication run between three quotes?
  2. For high-value items, three quotes are expected unless the supplier is a sole-source with justification.
  3. Open the adjudication section (if present) and confirm the chosen supplier is the winner on price, quality, or delivery.

6.4 Budget impact

  1. Open Budgets (Sidebar → Financial Planning → Budgets) in a new tab.
  2. Check the department's current spend versus budget.
  3. Ask yourself: if I approve this, how much headroom is left in the department's budget for the rest of the quarter?

6.5 Strategic check

  1. Does the purchase fit company strategy or a board commitment?
  2. Is this a one-off, or one of several similar items you'd expect to see?
  3. Is it visible to external stakeholders (auditors, regulators)?
PR detail view showing three-quote comparison (adjudication result) and budget impact.

7. Task 3 — Final Approve or Reject

7.1 Write a meaningful comment

  1. Scroll to the bottom.
  2. A one-sentence approval comment is usually enough — but make it count. Examples:
    • Approve: "Approved. Aligned with Q2 IT upgrade plan; vendor is preferred tier-1."
    • Approve with caveat: "Approved on condition the PO is issued this week to meet Required Date."
    • Reject: "Rejected. Adjudication is not compelling — please get a fourth quote from a tier-1 vendor."
    • Hold: leave a comment and do not click either button yet. Contact the HOD for clarification.

7.2 Click the button

  1. Approve: green button. Status becomes approved, Procurement is notified, the initiator and HOD receive email.
  2. Reject: red button. Status becomes rejected, and everyone down the chain sees the rejection reason.
Your action is final. There is no further escalation above you. Take the time you need before clicking.

8. Task 4 — Oversight Reports

8.1 Analytics (monthly)

  1. Sidebar → Reports & Analytics → Analytics.
  2. Pick date range: This Month or Last Month.
  3. Review:
    • Spend by department — any outliers?
    • Vendor concentration — are we over-reliant on one supplier?
    • Approval-chain bottlenecks — where are requests stalling?
    • Rejection rate — which HODs are rejecting most?

8.2 Summary Reports

  1. Sidebar → Reports & Analytics → Reports.
  2. Use the "Last Month" range for board-pack numbers.
  3. Export to PDF and attach to your monthly report to the Board.

8.3 Individual PR PDFs

  1. Open any approved PR.
  2. Click Download PDF.
  3. Use for signatures if a physical copy is required for external parties.

9. Task 5 — Delegation During Absence

9.1 Before you travel or take leave

  1. Tell your Administrator at least 48 hours in advance.
  2. Decide who can approve on your behalf — typically the Finance Manager or the Chair of the Board.
  3. Admin temporarily changes the MD threshold or grants approval rights to the delegate for that period.

9.2 After you return

  1. Ask Admin to remove the delegation.
  2. Review the Audit Log for approvals made during your absence. Any disagreements should be raised promptly.
The system keeps a full audit trail of who approved what and when, including during delegation periods. There is no way to hide or back-date an approval.

10. Troubleshooting

10.1 "Pending Approvals" list is empty — is everything approved?

  1. Possibly. Most requests never reach you because they're below the MD threshold.
  2. Check the "Approved Requisitions" list to confirm recent activity.
  3. If concerned, ask Finance what the current MD threshold is.

10.2 I approved by mistake

  1. Call the initiator and Procurement immediately — they may still be able to cancel the purchase order before sending.
  2. You cannot "un-approve" in the system. The record stands.
  3. If funds must be recovered, raise a separate issue with Finance.

10.3 The total amount shown looks wrong

  1. Multi-currency requests are converted using the current FX rate.
  2. Open FX Rates and check the rates are fresh.
  3. If conversion is stale, Finance should update rates — the total will refresh.

10.4 I want to see ALL approved requests for the month

  1. Sidebar → Procurement → Approved Requisitions.
  2. Filter by date range "This Month" or "Last Month".
  3. Export to Excel for analysis.

10.5 I need to contact the initiator but don't know them

  1. On the PR detail page, the Initiator field is clickable — it opens their user profile with email address.
  2. Or ask Admin for their contact details.

11.2 Your typical weekly rhythm

  • Monday: clear pending queue from the weekend.
  • Wednesday: spot-check the analytics for the previous week.
  • Friday: clear pending queue before the weekend.
  • End of month: export analytics to PDF for your monthly oversight note.

11.3 Delegation log format (suggested)

When delegating during an absence, record in the handover note:

  • Dates of absence.
  • Delegate name and role.
  • Any approval limit you want them to observe (e.g. "do not approve over K500k without calling me").
